Complete HVAC Overhaul for Orem Medical Office

Orem, Utah • 6,000 sq ft Medical Practice • Phased Replacement

A medical practice operating with three aging rooftop units faced comfort inconsistencies, high energy costs, and air quality concerns. Air Express designed a phased replacement that kept the practice fully operational while delivering 39% energy savings and superior patient comfort.

The Numbers

  • 39% energy savings
  • $8,400 annual savings
  • $1,800 → $1,100 monthly cost reduction
  • 3.8 years ROI
  • 3 units replaced
  • 0 hours practice closure

The Challenge

A successful medical practice in Orem had built its reputation on professional care and attention to detail. But their HVAC system was undermining that reputation. Three rooftop units, each over 12 years old, were struggling to maintain the precise temperature and air quality control that a medical office requires.

The problems were manifold. Exam rooms in one section of the building were consistently 4-5 degrees warmer than the front reception area. Patients commented on the inconsistent comfort. Winter heating bills topped $1,800 per month, and summer cooling costs were nearly as high. The practice manager knew something had to change, but the thought of closing for a complete overhaul was unacceptable during the busy medical season.

Beyond comfort issues, there were deeper concerns. Aging rooftop equipment creates air quality risks. The office staff worried about virus transmission and pollutants. Medical practices need special attention to air filtration and purification—the very thing these aging units couldn't provide.

The practice called Air Express for a consultation, expecting to hear about a multi-week nightmare of closures and reconstruction. Instead, they learned about a phased replacement strategy that would maintain operations throughout the project.

The Solution

Air Express didn't propose ripping out all three units at once. Instead, we designed a phased replacement strategy that could be executed over three consecutive weekends, keeping the practice fully operational during business hours.

The strategy was sophisticated. We analyzed cooling and heating loads, mapped air distribution patterns throughout the 6,000 square foot space, and identified which zones could be served by temporary equipment while permanent units were being installed. Each unit replacement was scheduled for a Friday afternoon through Sunday evening, allowing time for installation, testing, and startup verification.

We selected three high-efficiency commercial rooftop units specifically engineered for medical facilities. These weren't residential units scaled up—they were professional-grade equipment with redundancy, diagnostics, and the precision control that a medical office demands.

The installation included a sophisticated zoning system with individual dampers in each exam room, allowing precise temperature control in patient areas. Reception, administrative spaces, and clinical areas could each maintain optimal conditions. This eliminated the hot and cold spots that had plagued the practice.

Perhaps most importantly, Air Express integrated UV air purification technology into the system. Ultraviolet light in the ductwork kills mold, bacteria, and viruses as air circulates through the system. For a medical practice, this was a game-changing upgrade that reassured both staff and patients about air safety.

A modern building management system was integrated, allowing the practice manager to monitor and adjust temperatures remotely, view real-time energy consumption, and receive alerts if equipment performance drifts from expected parameters.

The Results

The transformation was immediate and measurable. After the first complete billing cycle with all three new units operational, the practice's utility costs dropped from $1,800 per month to $1,100. That's $8,400 in annual savings—money that could be reinvested in patient care, staff training, or facility improvements.

Energy savings weren't the only benefit. The inconsistent temperatures vanished. Every exam room now maintained the precise temperature set by the system. Reception areas felt welcoming. Clinical spaces stayed cool and comfortable regardless of the outdoor temperature.

Patient comments shifted. Instead of complaints about comfort, staff heard positive feedback. The practice began featuring the new system in patient communications, emphasizing the commitment to air safety and infection control through UV purification. This became a subtle but powerful marketing advantage in a competitive healthcare market.

The phased installation approach proved brilliant. By completing the project over three weekends, the practice never closed. There was no disruption to patient schedules, staff routines, or revenue. In fact, the practice continued normal operations during the entire process.

Most dramatically, the return on investment was stunning. The entire project—three rooftop units, controls, zoning system, UV purification, and professional installation—would pay for itself in just 3.8 years based on energy savings alone. After that, the practice would enjoy two decades of lower operating costs with modern equipment.

Our patients notice the difference immediately—the temperature is consistent, the air feels fresher, and we can tell them honestly that we've invested in advanced air purification for their safety. Commercial HVAC Lessons from This Project

This project demonstrates why choosing the right HVAC partner matters for commercial applications:

Operational continuity requires planning. Many HVAC contractors approach replacements with a "rip and replace" mentality. They remove the old system and install the new one, full stop. That works for residential homes but creates disaster for commercial operations. Air Express planned this replacement to maintain operations throughout, treating the practice's business continuity as a core requirement.

Zoning systems transform building performance. Many medical practices operate with basic single-zone systems—one thermostat, one set of temperatures. Modern zoning allows each space to maintain its optimal conditions. Exam rooms, waiting areas, administrative offices, and labs can all have independent controls, improving comfort and efficiency.

Medical facilities have special air quality needs. Standard air filters aren't enough in healthcare settings. UV purification, high-MERV filters, and humidity control all contribute to a safe environment. Air Express recognized this and integrated purification technology that many HVAC contractors wouldn't think to propose.

Building management systems are essential for large facilities. Remote monitoring, automated diagnostics, and performance trending help facility managers understand how equipment is performing and catch problems before they become emergencies. For a 6,000 square foot practice, this visibility is invaluable.

Why Commercial HVAC Differs from Residential

This case study reveals why commercial HVAC projects require different expertise:

  • Load calculations are more complex. A medical office has different cooling and heating needs than a home. Operating hours, internal heat generation from equipment, occupancy patterns—all must be factored into sizing decisions.
  • Rooftop units present access and safety challenges. Professional installation requires fall protection, equipment lifts, and coordination with building maintenance. This is different from residential systems.
  • Downtime has financial implications. When a residential furnace fails, a family gets uncomfortable. When a commercial HVAC system fails, a business loses revenue. Projects must be planned to minimize disruption.
  • Air quality requirements are higher. Medical offices, dental practices, and other healthcare facilities must meet specific air quality standards. Standard residential HVAC won't suffice.
  • Zoning and controls require expertise. Modern commercial systems integrate with building automation, provide remote monitoring, and support complex scheduling. Installation and setup demand specialized knowledge.
