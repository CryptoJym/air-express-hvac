OUR STORY · FAMILY-OWNED · LEHI, UTAH

Three generations.
One family.
One promise.

We started in 1996 with two technicians, one van, and a handshake promise: show up on time, tell the truth, fix it right. Nearly thirty years later, our kids work here. Our neighbors call us by name. And we still answer the phone.

1996 founded
3 generations
13 communities
280+ families

In 1996, two brothers drove a single van around Utah County looking for anyone who needed a furnace fixed.

The pitch was simple: we'd show up on time, we'd tell you what was wrong in plain English, and we'd charge what it cost — not what we could get away with. People didn't believe it at first. Some still don't until we actually leave their house without an upsell.

Nearly thirty years later, that van has become a fleet. The brothers brought in their kids. Those kids brought in their friends. We've weathered Utah's brutal winters and its scorching summers, the housing boom, the recession, the pandemic, the supply chain shocks. We've stayed right here in Lehi.

We're not a corporation. We're not a call center with a national 800 number. We're a family company that trains its own technicians, answers its own phones, and gets to know the houses we work on over decades. Most of our customers today first called us because a neighbor handed them our number at a school pickup line.

That's the business we built. That's the business we want to keep.

“Show up on time, tell the truth, fix it right.”

— The handshake promise, Lehi, 1996

WHAT WE STAND FOR

Six things we won't compromise on.

These aren't values we put on a conference room wall. They're the reasons our customers call us back the second time, and the third, and the tenth.

01

Honesty

We'll tell you when a repair doesn't make sense. We'll tell you when a replacement does. We'll tell you if a competitor's quote is actually fair. We'd rather lose the job than lie to you.

02

Reliability

Heat fails in January. AC dies in July. Neither of those things care what time it is. We answer the phone at 2am in February the same way we answer it at noon in June.

03

Craftsmanship

A bad install costs you money every month for fifteen years. A good one pays you back the same way. We size the job, install it right, and test it before we leave.

04

Community

We live in the neighborhoods we serve. Our kids play on the teams we sponsor. When we mess something up, we don't get to disappear — we have to look you in the eye at the grocery store.

05

Sustainability

Efficient systems, proper refrigerant handling, honest conversations about load calcs. Utah's air quality is already fragile. We're not going to make it worse for a sale.

06

Apprenticeship

We train our own technicians — paid, from day one, in the shop. The trades are how families used to build real lives in Utah. We want that back.

THE ROAD HERE

Thirty years, one Zip code.

  1. 1996

    Two brothers, one van

    Air Express starts in a Lehi garage with two techs, a used Chevy van, and a loan from a family friend. First job: a cracked heat exchanger on a 30-year-old furnace in American Fork.

  2. 2005

    Across Utah County

    The fleet grows to five vans. We expand into Eagle Mountain, Saratoga Springs, and Orem. Still family-owned. Still answering our own phones.

  3. 2010

    Paid apprenticeships

    We launch a formal apprenticeship program to train the next generation of Utah HVAC technicians. First two apprentices are still with us — one now runs a crew.

  4. 2015

    The review wall fills up

    We pass 100 five-star reviews on Google. We also get our first one-star review — a legitimate one. We reach out, fix it, and learn from it.

  5. 2020

    Showing up through everything

    The pandemic doesn't stop Utah furnaces from breaking. Our techs mask up, show up, and keep families warm through the worst winter of the decade.

  6. Today

    Still local. Still yours.

    Thirteen Wasatch Front communities. 280+ five-star reviews. Three generations of the same family answering the phone. The next chapter is still being written — same way as the first one.

COMMUNITY COMMITMENT

The part of the job no one pays us for.

Youth sports

Soccer, football, basketball, wrestling. Our logos are on the jerseys of half the elementary school teams in Lehi and Eagle Mountain. It's not marketing — it's our kids' teams.

Vocational education

We donate HVAC training systems to local high schools and partner with the UVU trades program. The best way to fix the trades shortage is to teach kids the trades are worth doing.

Utah air quality

Proper refrigerant reclamation, EPA-certified handling, high-efficiency installs. Utah's inversion winters are bad enough without HVAC contractors cutting corners. We don't.

Neighbors in need

Every year we quietly cover emergency heating bills for families who can't. No paperwork, no press release, no social media post. Just a working furnace on a cold January night.
