Factors That Affect Your HVAC Costs in Utah

Home Size & Square Footage

Larger homes require more powerful systems. A 2,000 sq ft home costs significantly more to heat/cool than a 1,000 sq ft home. Utah's wide elevation range means some homes need oversized units.

System Age & Condition

Systems over 12-15 years old cost more to repair. Older equipment uses outdated parts, and major repairs often exceed replacement costs. Air Express evaluates repair vs. replacement value.

Utah Altitude & Climate

Utah's 4,000-9,000 ft elevation affects system performance. Mountain areas need larger capacity units. Seasonal extremes (hot summers, cold winters) impact equipment wear and replacement frequency.

Ductwork Condition

Leaky or damaged ducts reduce efficiency by 20-40%. Duct sealing or replacement adds to total project cost. Utah's dry air increases dust accumulation in ducts.

Energy Efficiency Rating (SEER/AFUE)

High-efficiency systems (SEER 18+, AFUE 95%+) cost more upfront but save 15-30% on utility bills over 10+ years. Federal tax credits offset some costs.

Complexity of Installation

Systems requiring new ductwork, gas line extensions, or roof work cost more. Simple replacements (like-for-like) are cheaper. Air Express designs efficient layouts for lower total cost.
